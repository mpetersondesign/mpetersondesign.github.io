What is Dragon Quest?


Dragon Quest, initially known as "Dragon Warrior" in North America, is a japanese role-playing-game developed for home consoles and handhelds by Square-Enix. Dragon Quest is credited to be the mother of all modern turn-based RPGs, revolutionizing the market with its release and inspiring its successors, such as the ever popular (but not nearly as influential or fun) Final Fantasy franchise.


Starting with its release on the NES in 1986, Dragon Quest has trekked through 30 years of video game history, with it's most recent installment having landed in 2017. Despite only having 11 games, Dragon Quest has survived through the years by its ever popular spin offs and side content media, such as arcade exclusives and prefecture-wide soundtrack concerts.


Dragon Quest has had little traction in the west over it's lifespan, only picking up attention in the more recent years, however, in Japan, "Dragon Quest" is a title every household knows, going as far as to dedicate a national holiday every day a new game is released, with many companies giving employees a free day to go buy and try the new game. To this end, Dragon Quest has spawned its own Shounen Jump! manga, an anime based off of that manga, it's own spin-off anime, and more recently, it's own full length feature-film.
